Act IV · Craft

The shape of the craft

What threads every house — a shared lexicon, a steady method.

Identity
Cards of essence
Every entity in the mesh carries an identity card — names, lineages, provenance. The mesh joins on essence, never on guess.
Lexicon
Three axes of mark
The marks that travel with the work are kept along three axes of provenance:
  • WHAT — the substance itself
  • HOW — the method of its making
  • WHERE — the lineage of its origin
Philosophy
Four temperaments
The disposition of every work in the mesh:
  • Layered — mechanical kept apart from editorial
  • Observable — every fact carries its provenance
  • Repeatable — the same hand, the same line
  • Interpretive — the angle is named, not hidden
Method
Contract before code
Every work begins with its contract. Before the line is drawn, the shape is set; before the hand moves, the why is written.
Naming
A mythic lexicon
Names from older books thread the mesh:
  • Apollo — the light of knowledge
  • Atlas — the bearer of charts
  • Argus — the hundred-eyed watcher
  • Eidos — the form, the essence
  • Lumin · Alchemy — light and transmutation
Posture
Patient instruments
We make instruments, not announcements. The work in the mesh is read by other works — quiet, joined, kept under one craft.
